## 1. Thesis type

Two types exist. Ours = **Type 1 (system development)** — design, implementation,
test, and evaluation of a complete system (PowerFlowJulia), with strong Type-2
(research/benchmarking investigation) elements. Frame primarily as Type 1.

## 2. Required document structure (recommended, not strictly prescriptive)

Default IU body structure:
1. Title page *(not counted in page numbering display; counted in count)*
2. Table of contents (with page numbers)
3. Tables of figures and terms *(optional)*
4. Abstract (informative)
5. **Body:**
   - Introduction
   - Literature Review
   - Design & Methodology
   - Implementation & Results
   - **Analysis and Discussion**  ← we currently fold this into other chapters
   - Conclusion
6. Bibliography / References
7. Appendices

Formal admission (docx) also requires the **Foreword/Introduction to explicitly
contain**: relevance, purpose, tasks, study object, scientific novelty.

> "Recommended, not prescriptive" — supervisor may request a particular structure.

## 3. Page length

- **40+ pages** minimum.
- The 40+ **includes**: table of contents + thesis chapters.
- The 40+ **excludes**: title page, appendices, reference list.
- (docx doc states "not less than 40 pages".) Our current draft ≈ 66 pp → OK.

## 4. Abstract

- **One paragraph.**
- ⚠️ Length: PPT says **150–250 words**; Guidelines say **max 400 words**.
  → target **~250 words** (satisfies both).
- Must be submitted in **English AND Russian**.
- Must contain, in the same order as the thesis: research gap; limitations of
  previous research; novelty; research objective; research method; research
  results; result implications/discussion.
- Tenses: same as corresponding chapters, or present tense throughout.

## 5. Formatting (from docx methodical recommendations)

- Font **Times New Roman 14 pt**, line spacing **1.5**.
- Margins: **left 25 mm, right/top/bottom 20 mm**.
- First-line indent **1.25**; justified body.
- Each chapter/section/foreword/conclusion/appendix **starts on a new page**.
- **Continuous page numbering**; title page counted but unnumbered.
- Figure caption **below** the figure (left, with number).
- Table caption **above** the table (centered).
- Paged links + references placed at the end.

## 6. Visuals (from 2022 Guidelines — detailed)

**General:** place visual near (preferably after) first mention; progress
vertically; understandable from caption alone; always interpret in text.

**Figures (3.12.3):**
- Caption **below** the figure.
- Abbreviate "Figure" → **"Fig."**, em-space after "Fig.".
- Number **continuously** throughout; **period after the number**.
- Capitalize first word; **no articles** at start; **period at end**.
- Multi-part: (a), (b), (c)… preceding each part.
- Label everything; explain symbols/abbreviations; **units in parentheses** on axes, e.g. "Voltage (V)".
- Refer using Present Simple.

**Tables (3.12.4):**
- Caption **above** the table.
- Use label **"TABLE" + Roman numerals** (TABLE I, TABLE II…), centered.
- Caption as an inverted pyramid; capitalize nouns/verbs/adjectives/adverbs and prepositions >3 letters.
- Multi-part: (a),(b),(c)…
- **No period** at end of table caption.

**Equations (3.12.5):**
- Part of the text; numbered **continuously, in brackets**, **flush right**.
- Refer as "Equation" at sentence start, else just the number, e.g. "in (1)".
- End with period if it concludes a sentence; delete trailing commas.

## 7. Digits & numbers (3.13)

- Never start a sentence with a digit.
- Spell out numbers **below 11** ("seven experiments"); digits for >10.
- Use digits with units ("33 min."); abbreviate units with quantities.
- Spell out units used without quantities.

## 8. Lists (3.14)

- Labeling order: `1) … a) … i) …`.
- Run-in lists for ≤3 items, separated by semicolons, introduced by a colon.
- Displayed lists for ≥3 items.
- Where-lists define equation variables (indent LHS, lowercase RHS, semicolons; period on last).

## 9. References / Bibliography

- **IEEE style**, numbered **[1]**, flush left, hanging indent.
- In-text: **"in [1]"**, **"Smith et al. [5] claim…"**, **"According to [3]…"** — never "in reference [1]" or "In Smith [1]".
- Minimum **30 sources** (docx + guidelines).
- Include every source used; provide in-text citation for every source mentioned.
- Avoid non-credible sources: Wikipedia, non-peer-reviewed sites, non-academic manuscripts, blogs, social networks.
- **Never use Russian-style quotation marks** («…»; „…").
- IEEE reference guide: https://journals.ieeeauthorcenter.ieee.org/

## 10. Anti-plagiarism

- ⚠️ Guidelines: authenticity **below 70%** → returned for revision.
  (docx: "if insufficient" — unquantified.) **User target: ≥ 75%.**
- "No plagiarism is acceptable." Explain issues in own words; do not recycle text.

## 11. Tenses by chapter (3.x)

- Introduction: Present Simple (aims); Present Perfect (background).
- Literature Review: Past Simple / Present Simple / Present Perfect Simple.
- Design & Methodology: Past Simple (completed work); Present Simple for math/derivations.
- Implementation & Results: **Past Simple only** (results obtained in the past).
- Analysis & Discussion: Present Simple; modals of possibility (might/could/may).
- Conclusion: Present Perfect (looking back); Past Simple (methods/results); Present Simple (significance).

## 12. Title rules

- Concise, informative, searchable.
- Capitalize every noun, verb, adjective, adverb, subordinating conjunction;
  prepositions >3 letters capitalized; otherwise lowercase.

## 13. Submission for defense (admission)

Submit to Department of Education (≥10 working days before defense):
- Bound thesis, **signed by supervisor on the title page**, per requirements doc.
- ⚠️ **Thesis annotation in Russian**: docx says **4–5 pages**; Guidelines say **10–15 pages**. → CONFIRM with supervisor.
- Thesis + abstract soft copy as **PDF with a text layer** uploaded to Moodle. (docx also allows .docx.)
- Supervisor's review (signed).
- External expert assessment (**masters only**).
- Presentation slides + handouts (if applicable).
- Final Thesis Assessment grading form.

## 14. Defense / presentation

- Defense in **English**; **≤ 20 min** total (docx) — Guidelines: **10 min talk + 10 min Q&A**.
- First slide: full name, thesis title, supervisor's full name.
- Recommended slide flow: context → open problem/RQs → lit review/background →
  proposed solution (design & methodology) → implementation & experiments →
  results & discussion → conclusions (contribution, impact, future work).
- Convert to PDF; send to education@innopolis.ru 1–2 days before defense.

## 15. Assessment criteria (Type 1 highlights)

- Design meets requirements/specs; theoretical model used/developed; formal design
  methodology adopted and used; well-structured (hierarchical, clear interfaces,
  functional spec per sub-system); fully implemented; modular; high quality
  (elegant/robust, industry-standard).
- Organization & clarity, technical mastery, own words (no recycled text),
  conclusions significance, presentation delivery.
